Dr. Zifeng Chen received his B.Eng. degree from Shenyang University of Technology, his M.Sc. degree from Newcastle University, United Kingdom, and his Ph.D. degree from The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology in 2026. He is affiliated with the Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering, School of Engineering, at The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology. He was also a Visiting Scientist in Mechanical Engineering at University College London, where he worked on high-performance servo drive systems.

His research interests include motor drives and control, sensorless control of electric machines, switched reluctance motor drives, robotic joint servo systems, and transportation electrification. He has published more than 20 papers in leading journals and flagship conferences in electrical engineering, including IEEE Transactions on Transportation Electrification, IEEE Transactions on Industrial Electronics, and IEEE Transactions on Power Electronics. He also serves as the co-session chair of “Robotic Joint Motor Drives and Health Monitoring” at ICEMS 2026.

Dr. Chen has received several academic honors and awards, including the HKUST FUNH Ph.D. Research Excellence Award, the Best Paper Award at IEEE CIEEC 2024, and the Gold Award at the 51st International Exhibition of Inventions Geneva.


Research Interests
======
My research focuses on advanced motor drives and control for high-performance electromechanical systems. I am particularly interested in sensorless control of electric machines, switched reluctance motor drives, robotic joint servo systems, and transportation electrification. My work aims to improve the dynamic performance, robustness, efficiency, and practical deployability of electric drive systems for emerging applications in robotics, electric vehicles, and electric aviation.

Current research topics include:

· Sensorless control and position estimation of electric machines

· High-performance servo drive systems for robotic joints

· Rare-earth-free motor drives and control

· Advanced control methods for motor drive systems

· Electric propulsion and transportation electrification

· Fault diagnosis and health monitoring of electric drive systems
